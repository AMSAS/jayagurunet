Saraswata Math

Assam Bangiya Saraswat Matha (previously known as Shanti Ashram) is a monastic organization founded by Srimat Swami Nigamananda Saraswati Paramahansa Deva in 1912 AD. The main aim behind the foundation of this Matha is to spread and propagate Sanatana Dharma and true knowledge about God, besides preparing the inmates for achieving spiritual attainments for undertaking the job of Guru.

Shanti Ashram, Kokilamukh, Assam, India [Established - 1912]

Purchasing an 80 bighas plot at Kumarveti Chapri village of Kokilamukh from Assam (Eastern State of India) Government, the Math was instituted by Shri Shri Thakura with the asan of Gurubrhama in the propitious occasion of Akshay Tritiya on 7th Baisakh 1319 B.S. and named "Shanti Ashram".

First seven self denying disciples (Swami Chidananda, Swami Premananda, Swami Swarupananda, Swami Yogananda, Swami Suddhananda, Swami Bodhananda and Swami Saradananda) were initiated asceticism by Shri Shri Thakura and he gave name to this Ashram as "Saraswata Matha". Since 1325 B.S. it had been named as "Assam Bangiya Saraswat Math". Being an ascetic relating to the title of Saraswati under the Sringeri Math he gives the name to his Matha as 'Saraswata Matha', Sangha as 'Saraswata Sangha', books compiled by him as 'Saraswata Granthabali'.

"I wish to awaken my dispirited people through Dharma; to manifest in them the ideals of the great Rishi-Age and to bestow humanity with their loftiest gift; the gift of Self-Knowledge. This is the ideal of our Sangha." - Swami Nigamananda
